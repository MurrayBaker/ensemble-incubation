South London Community Music is a community music charity that brings people together through music. We aim to:

1. support our ensembles
1. engage our community
1. promote inclusion

We provide resources and governance for three existing community groups:

* [South London Jazz Orchestra (SLJO)](https://sljo.org.uk) - a jazz big band
* [South London Symphonic Winds (SLSW)](https://slsw.org.uk) - a wind orchestra
* [Geeky Beats](https://geekybeatsbigband.co.uk) - a videogame music big band 

We've been operating in South London for 20+ years.

We're based at [Mostyn Road Methodist Church](https://maps.app.goo.gl/58awTcMFXi1BgGJk9), near Brixton.

We are looking for a new ensemble to join our community.

## What We're Looking For

You're an existing ensemble, or you have a firm plan to form one. You're one of the following:

* Instrumental ensemble (e.g. brass band, string orchestra) 
* Community Choir (Rock/Gospel/Chamber)
* Music education group/collective
* Other music-related thing, let's discuss! 

You're formed of your local community, and don't operate for profit. Your aims align with ours: you provide a safe, inclusive, and encouraging space where people take joy in music together.

## Long-Term Benefits of SLCM Membership

* Regular rehearsal space (with plenty of storage), reasonable rates
* Charitable status
* Governance framework
* Gear - drums, percussion, and recording and amplification equipment (subject to specific arrangement)
* Connection with ~180 other community musicians. Dep networks and enthusiastic audiences.

You'd be expected to take on one slot per week. Mondays are available, 5-10pm, flexibly.

## What We Need From You

To get the conversation started we need to know:

* Who you are and what do you do? What music do you make?
* What makes your group a good fit for our values?
* An explanation of how you (plan to) engage with the community.

Long-term the payment structure depends on the size of your ensemble and your resources, but you'd expect to be contributing in the region of £300 per month for space, storage, and insurance. If you're not established we can be flexible with costs while you grow your membership. This is considered on a case-by-case basis.

This isn't an intermittent one-off deal, we're looking for a long-term engagement. The new ensemble(s) ideally join SLCM as full members, including assigning a trustee to the board. In our last ensemble onboarding this took twelve months.

[Send us an email](mailto:slcmchair@gmail.com) with those details and we'll be in touch!

Applications are considered on a rolling basis, but we're aiming to onboard as soon as possible.
